# BTX MatMul v4.4 — Hardware Economics Inversion: Grounded Design Study

*Date: 2026-07-20. Status: research synthesis + design recommendation. Non-normative.*

## 0. Problem statement

A mining cost-efficiency benchmark of the ENC-DR / ENC-DR-LT workload showed the
economics **inverted the wrong way**: measured in knonce per rental-dollar, a
consumer RTX 5090 was the *most* cost-efficient miner and a datacenter B200 the
*least*. The goal of this study was to determine — with every consensus,
parameter, and workload option on the table (hard fork) — whether the workload
can be reshaped so that **datacenter HBM-class silicon wins cost-per-hash while
smaller machines remain able to participate (as idle compute), just
uneconomically**. I.e. tax the small machines without excluding them.

This memo records the grounded result of six independent hardware-economics
research passes (mid-2026 data) plus an adversarial grounding critique, folds in
the "Remembered Palace" bandwidth-hardening construction, and states an honest,
buildable recommendation. It supersedes the earlier optimistic framing that a
graceful workload lever could reach the rental break-even against a 5090.

The two standing impossibility results from prior exploration are used as
guardrails throughout:

- **§L.4 (no sound hard gate):** in a nonce-parallel PoW, no *hard* resource gate
  (refuse-to-run below some capacity) is sound, because a miner shards nonces
  across many small devices. Only *soft* economic penalties are admissible.
- **Anti-correlation theorem:** a workload cannot simultaneously deliver a large
  (>~22× rental) hardware separation *and* ε=0 exact determinism when the
  separation is sought on the **arithmetic/precision** axis — the two levers
  anti-correlate.

The key advance below is that the resident-reuse mechanism seeks separation on a
**third axis — I/O cost-to-compute** — which is orthogonal to both guardrails: it
is a soft tax (not a hard gate) and it separates on cost, not on the arithmetic
result (so bit-exactness is preserved). That is why it is worth pursuing. Its
*magnitude*, however, is bounded by concrete silicon facts, derived below.

## 1. The Remembered Palace — bandwidth-hardening reference construction

Reframing the PoW as "a puzzle an AI system plays to keep its mind fresh between
other work" produced a clean, minimal construction (the *Remembered Palace*) that
is, decoded, a **data-dependent memory-hard function (dMHF) of the scrypt/ROMix
family** wrapped in a spot-checkable opening transcript. It is retained here as
the **bandwidth-hardening reference**, because its verification structure and its
"recall beats re-derivation" property are directly reusable.

**Board.** Fix a public hash `H`. Each round has a fresh seed. A palace of `N`
rooms is generated by a strictly sequential chain:

```
r_0 = H(seed)      r_{k+1} = H(r_k ‖ k)   for k = 0 … N−1
```

Room `k`'s "sigil" is the first 48 bits of `r_k`. The chain is unshortcuttable:
room `k` is only derivable by walking `0…k−1`. Rooms are pseudorandom, hence
incompressible (~48·N-bit floor); a forgotten room can only be re-derived by
replaying from the nearest remembered earlier room.

**Workout.** `W` couriers each take `T` adaptive random-access hops:

```
c_0^w = H(seed ‖ "courier" ‖ w)
p_t = c_t mod N       (visit room p_t)
c_{t+1} = H(c_t ‖ sigil(p_t))
```

The next room depends on the current room's contents (data-dependent access), so
the pattern cannot be streamed or prefetched. Seal `A = H(a_1 ‖ … ‖ a_W)`.

**Why bigger memory is smoothly faster.** Holding fraction `f = m/N` of the
palace, each hop hits memory with probability `f`; a miss costs `≈ N/2m` re-dream
steps. Expected per-hop cost `f·ρ + (1−f)·(N/2m)` is smooth and continuous in `m`
— no cliff, no threshold. Illustrative (`N`=10⁶, `W`=512, `T`=4096): full-palace
≈ effortless; 1%-held ≈ 35× slower; 0.1%-held ≈ 500× slower — but it *finishes*
with the identical `A`.

**Verification (reusable).** Solver submits `A` plus a transcript of claimed
sigils. Checker: (1) replay the walk arithmetic `O(W·T)`; (2) sort claimed
`(position, sigil)` pairs — this collapses the *adaptive* access into a *static*
list; (3) stream the corridor once, `O(N)` sequential hashes, constant memory,
checking each claimed sigil as its room scrolls past. **Solving is online random
access (memory-hard); checking is offline streaming (memory-free).** A 1%
spot-check mode gives probabilistic certainty cheaply. This is exactly BTX's
existing Merkle-opening + Fiat-Shamir spot-check architecture (k≈76/f),
independently rederived — so the Palace *validates* the current verification
design and supplies the transcript structure for verifying the multi-pass
workload in §5.

**Why it does not, by itself, invert the economics.** The Palace is ε=0 exact and
sound on small devices (they finish) — so by the anti-correlation theorem its
separation is graceful, not a cliff. Worse for our goal, its anti-cheat leans on
**serial** hash-chain latency ("recall beats re-derivation"), and serial latency
is the one axis on which datacenter and consumer silicon are *closest*. So the
Palace is the right *bandwidth-meter* skeleton but the wrong *economic lever*. The
lever must come from resident-reuse bandwidth (§5), not from serialization.

## 2. Six grounding findings (mid-2026)

All figures well-sourced; sources listed in §8.

**F1 — On-chip memory census.** B200: 126 MB L2 (physically 2×63 MB, cross-die
penalty above 63 MB), 228 KB shared/SM, **256 KB/SM TMEM (~38 MB total,
datacenter-exclusive; measured L2 BW ~16.8–21 TB/s, HBM3e ~8 TB/s → L2 buys only
~2.1× over its own HBM)**. **The RTX 5090 has a 96 MB L2 — nearly equal to B200's
126 MB and larger than B200's 38 MB TMEM.** On-chip capacity ratio B200:5090 is
single-digit. Effective B200:5090 ≈ 6–10×; spill graceful (~1.5–2×), never a
cliff. B200:Mac ≈ 100–400× but from missing FP4/FP8 hardware, not SRAM.
Trainium2: 28 MB SBUF; TPU v6e: tens-of-MB VMEM. **[Correction from grounding
pass: MI355X has 256 MB Infinity Cache + ~40 MB LDS, NOT 64 MB — the only
datacenter on-chip pool decisively larger than the 5090's 96 MB L2.]**

**F2 — Roofline.** Datacenter chips have the *highest* ridge points (B200 ~563,
MI355X ~1263 OP/byte vs 5090 ~468, Mac ~40), so they are the *last* to go
compute-bound (B200 n≈844, 5090 n≈701, Mac n≈50–64). **There is no square-GEMM
shape where a B200 is compute-bound while a 5090/Mac is still bandwidth-bound.**
B200:5090 effective throughput is trapped in 4.5×–5.4× for all n; the crossover
only *shrinks* the lead.

**F3 — Rental economics.** B200 ~$5.5/hr (floor ~$3), H200 ~$3–4, MI355X
~$2.6–3.5, 5090 ~$0.55 (floor ~$0.27), 3090 ~$0.22. B200:5090 premium ~10–13×;
B200 delivers only 4–8× throughput → **5090 wins cost-efficiency by 1.25–3×.**
Break-even needs ~10–13× throughput. Macs are owner-operated (no rental market).
3090 lacks FP8/FP4, so B200 beats it on low precision.

**F4 — Architectural discontinuities.** The 5090 shares B200's gen-5 Tensor
Cores, native FP4/FP6/FP8, and single-CTA TMA — so **FP4 and TMA are NOT
datacenter-vs-consumer walls** (they only retire Ampere/Mac). Datacenter-exclusive
features: NVLink-5 coherent fabric, tcgen05/TMEM (undefeatable ISA wall, but only
~1.5–2.5× on GEMM), thread-block clusters/DSM (~few×). **[Corrected by grounding
pass: NVLink's 14–28× magnitude is real only for *communication-bound* workloads;
dense GEMM is partition-friendly, so it is NOT such a workload — see §4.]**

**F5 — I/O complexity (the crux).** Single GEMM traffic is Θ(n³/√M); the √M law
is a hard theorem (Loomis–Whitney caps arithmetic intensity at O(√M)), so a 100×
memory deficit costs only √100 = 10× more traffic — sublinear, un-steepenable for
one GEMM. **But a resident working set W reused across P dependent passes breaks
past √M**: if W fits in M_big but not M_small, the small machine re-streams W
every pass → *traffic* ratio ≈ P (unbounded). The message/latency metric is also
superlinear (n³/M^{3/2}).

**F6 — Precedent.** No verifiable dense-tensor workload has ever beaten consumer
GPUs by >20× on throughput-per-dollar. Pearl/PRL "datacenter favoring" is **pool
policy** (20%-fee H100/H200 official pool), *not* a silicon gate — a 3090 and an
H100 land in the same ~−70% ROI tier; community pools mine it on 3090s. The only
mechanism in the literature yielding a large asymmetry is a memory-capacity /
residency gate (arXiv 2602.09369), which works by **exclusion**, not per-FLOP
efficiency.

## 3. The resident-reuse mechanism, and why P cancels in the time ratio

The one genuine escape from the graceful-arithmetic ceiling is F5's resident-reuse
threshold, because it separates on **cost-to-compute (I/O), not on the result** —
threading both guardrails (§0). But its magnitude must be derived in *time*, not
*traffic*.

**Per-pass time model** (verified in grounding pass). Working set W; P dependent
passes; per-pass FLOPs F; one-time per-nonce cost L (generate/load W):

```
T_big(P)   = L_b + P · max(F/C_b,  W/B_on)          # W resident, on-chip BW B_on
T_small(P) = L_s + P · max(F/C_s,  t_mem,s)          # W re-streamed each pass
```

As `P → ∞`, `R(P) = T_small/T_big → r* = t_s/t_b`, **independent of P**. So:

- **Traffic ratio grows like P (F5 is right about traffic).**
- **Time/cost ratio saturates at the per-pass bottleneck ratio; P cancels.** Large
  P is *required* to amortize the per-nonce working-set generation L (it lifts
  R from ≈ the compute ratio toward r*), but it is an **amortizer, not an
  amplifier**. There is no regime where the time separation grows unboundedly with
  P: (i) state that grows per pass eventually evicts from the big machine too; (ii)
  the n³/M^{3/2} latency term does not rescue it (L2-vs-GDDR latency is only
  ~1.5–2×, and a dependent chain that exposes latency destroys throughput on
  *both* machines; restoring parallelism restores bandwidth-boundedness).

**Consequence.** The residency mechanism upgrades the separation axis from the
roofline **compute** gap (~5×, F2) to the **on-chip-BW(big) ÷ off-chip-BW(small)**
gap — real, ε=0-sound, and a soft tax. Its ceiling is a bandwidth ratio, not P.

## 4. Why the magnitude is capped against the flagship 5090

Two silicon facts bound the mechanism against the strongest consumer card, and
one economic fact undoes the fabric alternative.

**(a) The 96 MB-L2 cap (partial residency).** Because the 5090's 96 MB L2 is
nearly as large as a B200's 126 MB L2 and *larger* than B200's 38 MB TMEM, there
is **no working-set size resident on a B200 but not on a 5090**, except the narrow
96–126 MB band. In that band the 5090 plays partial residency — pin 96 MB in L2
(`cudaAccessPolicyWindow`) and stream only the remainder:

```
t_mem,s = W · [ (1−f)/B_off + f/B_L2,s ],   f = M_s/W
```

W = 120 MB, f = 0.80, B_off = 1.79 TB/s, B_L2,5090 ≈ 7 TB/s → effective 5090 BW
≈ 4.1 TB/s → r* vs B200's 16.8 TB/s ≈ **~4.1×** (not 4.5–9×). The honest
B200/H200-vs-5090 residency ceiling is **~4–7×**, below the 10–13× rental
break-even. Every NVIDIA workaround (TMEM-resident accumulation; sum-of-hierarchy
sets; the 96–126 MB band) collapses to ≤ ~7× or degenerates into a hard exclusion
gate barred by §L.4.

**(b) The NVLink/HBM-capacity gate fails for dense GEMM.** A working set exceeding
one GPU's HBM (>32 GB) superficially forces NVLink (8 TB/s) vs consumer PCIe
(63 GB/s) ≈ 125×, as a soft tax (a 5090 completes from host RAM). But **dense GEMM
is partition-friendly**: communication is O(n²) (or O(n²/√p)) per O(n³) flops, and
a row-partitioned `A·X` needs only a skinny-X allgather. A **4×5090 rig
($2.2/hr, 128 GB, 7.2 TB/s aggregate GDDR) nearly matches one B200 ($5.5/hr)**.
So NVLink's magnitude is unreachable for our workload; the only "fix" is a
single-device attestation rule — i.e. pool policy, the non-silicon gate F6 warns
against. A partial mitigation — mixing both rows *and* columns of the resident
state each pass, forcing per-pass cross-card reductions on the critical path —
blunts but does not eliminate multi-card L2 aggregation.

## 5. The strategic pivot: cache-per-dollar, not raw power → MI355X

The inversion is achievable, but **not** by making the most powerful chip win
(a B200 cannot beat its own 10–13× rental premium). It is achievable by designing
for the chip with the best **on-chip-cache-residency per rental-dollar**, which is
**AMD's MI355X**, because F1's corrected 256 MB Infinity Cache opens a
**96 → 256 MB residency band four times wider than B200's**, while MI355X's rental
premium over a 5090 is only ~4.7–6.4× (vs B200's 10–13×).

| vs RTX 5090 | Throughput | Rental premium | Cost-efficiency (DC ÷ consumer) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **B200** ($5.5/hr) | 4.3× | 10× | **0.43× — B200 loses 2.3×** |
| **MI355X** ($2.6–3.5/hr) | 6.7× | 4.7–6.4× | **1.0–1.4× — thin win** |

The MI355X win comes from a *smaller denominator*, not a bigger separation: at
W ≈ 240 MB the 5090's partial-residency benefit shrinks (f ≈ 0.39, effective
~2.5 TB/s) → r* ≈ 17/2.53 ≈ 6.7× against only a ~5–6× premium. And the tail is
taxed exactly as desired: **~1.5–2.6× vs a 3090, ~100× vs a Mac, with no
exclusion** (a Mac simply runs it slowly from unified memory).

### Tightest sound parameter set (from grounding pass)

Resident-state chained integer GEMM:

- **State X:** n × n INT8, **n = 15,616 → W ≈ 244 MB** (Infinity-Cache-resident on
  MI355X; cache-overflowing on 5090 / B200 / H200; trivially fits every VRAM and
  any Mac unified memory — soft tax, never exclusion).
- **Per pass:** `X ← (I + U_p V_pᵀ) · X · (I + R_p S_pᵀ)  (mod 251)`, with seeded
  thin `U,V,R,S` of rank **b = 64**. Per-pass FLOPs F = 512·n²; arithmetic
  intensity ≈ 256 op/byte — *below* the 5090's ~468 ridge (so 5090 stays
  memory-bound) and *above* the tail's ridges (so 3090/Mac go compute-bound).
  Two-sided mixing forces per-pass cross-card reductions (§4(b) mitigation).
- **Exactness:** INT32 accumulate, mod-251 requantize → bit-exact, deterministic,
  **ε=0**, Freivalds-verifiable in O(n²b) per pass; transcript/opening structure
  per the Palace (§1).
- **P = 1024** (amortizes per-nonce W-generation ≥100×; larger P is neutral, §3).
- **Per-nonce freshness:** W must be *nonce-derived* so it cannot be amortized
  across nonce attempts (else a small machine loads W once and reuses it across
  all its nonces, killing the tax). Generating W must be cheap relative to the
  P-pass workout.

Modeled per-pass times: MI355X 28.8 µs; B200 ~44.5 µs; 5090 193 µs; 3090
520–880 µs; M-series Mac ~2–4 ms.

## 6. Honest bottom line

- A sound, inclusive, ε=0 workload **cannot beat the flagship RTX 5090 by >13×**.
  Every residency construction collapses to ≤ ~7× vs the 5090, is eroded by
  multi-card L2 aggregation, or degenerates into an exclusion / single-device
  policy gate — consistent with F6's "nobody has ever exceeded 20×" precedent and
  with §L.4 + the anti-correlation theorem.
- **The economics inversion is nonetheless achievable in a narrower, defensible
  form:** design for **cache-per-rental-dollar** (datacenter AMD MI355X, W ≈
  240 MB resident-reuse), which yields a thin **1.0–1.4× cost-efficiency win vs
  the 5090**, **~1.5–2.6× vs a 3090**, and **~100× vs a Mac** — taxing the entire
  consumer tail while excluding no one. The >20× tail separation comes from the
  tail's low-precision **compute** deficit (missing FP4/FP8), not from residency.
- The resident-reuse mechanism is a genuine advance over "graceful ceiling only":
  it is the first lever found that is simultaneously ε=0-sound, §L.4-compliant
  (soft tax), and steeper than the roofline — because it separates on **I/O cost**,
  a third axis orthogonal to both impossibility results.

### Uncertainties to close before committing (flagged, not asserted)

- MI355X Infinity-Cache **bandwidth** (~17 TB/s assumed from MI300X lineage; not
  confirmed for CDNA4) — load-bearing for the 6.7× figure.
- MI355X INT8 **dense** rate (~10 POPS assumed at FP8 parity).
- 5090 L2 bandwidth (~7 TB/s estimate) — moves the partial-residency cap.
- 3090 dense-vs-sparse INT8 (142 vs 284 TOPS ambiguity) — moves the 3090 column ~2×.

## 7. Recommendation

1. **Do not** pursue any B200-favoring or NVLink-fabric gate: the former loses to
   its own rental premium; the latter fails on dense GEMM and/or becomes a policy
   gate. **Do not** market a >13×-vs-5090 separation; it is not achievable soundly.
2. **If a hard fork proceeds on economics grounds, target the MI355X cache band:**
   the §5 parameter set (INT8 resident state W ≈ 240 MB, rank-64 two-sided mixing
   mod-251, P = 1024, nonce-derived W), verified with the Palace transcript
   structure. Expect a thin, price-regime-dependent cost-efficiency win over the
   5090 and a clean tax on the tail — not a rout.
3. **Before committing, measure** the four flagged uncertainties on real MI355X /
   5090 silicon (Infinity-Cache BW, INT8 dense rate, 5090 L2 BW, 3090 INT8 mode).
   The 6.7× hinges on the first two.
4. **Governance framing:** the honest value proposition is "datacenter AMD wins on
   cost-efficiency, everyone else participates as idle compute" — inclusive by
   design, and the opposite of Bitcoin's two-ASIC-vendor concentration. Do not
   overstate it as "datacenter silicon dominates"; the flagship consumer card
   remains competitive, which is a *feature* for decentralization, not a bug.
