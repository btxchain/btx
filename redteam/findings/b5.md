# b5 — Reorg / selfish-mining / difficulty-timing economics on ASERT (D3/D4/A7)

Scope: empirical regtest on `btx-node:pr214-regtest-ruleon` (cap-OFF: `future_mtp_limit_height=INT32_MAX`
with the standard `-test=matmuldgw` args) plus a self-built **cap-ON** variant
(`rtb5-capon`: patched regtest to set `nMatMulMaxFutureMtpDriftHeight=2`, drift 3600). All runs used
`-regtestmatmulaserthalflife=3600` to match mainnet, half-life 3600 s, spacing 90 s, and `-mocktime`
to drive per-block timestamps. Namespace `rtB5-`/`rtb5-`, `/tmp/rtB5/`. All containers/network/variant
image cleaned up on exit.

## Ground-truth mechanism (code, then validated empirically)
- **ASERT is absolute-anchor aserti3-2d** (`src/pow.cpp:1542-1571`). Next target =
  `CalculateMatMulAsertTarget(anchor_target, time_diff = tip.time − anchor.time, height_diff = tip.height − anchor.height, half_life)`.
  It uses ONLY the tip's `(time, height)` vs a FIXED anchor — **path-independent / memoryless**: the
  difficulty for the block at height H is a pure function of block H-1's timestamp. Intermediate
  timestamps don't matter.
- Per-block factor `2^((time_diff − spacing·(height_diff+1))/half_life)`. **Measured vs predicted matched to 4 sig figs**
  (see A7). The regtest anchor sits essentially at powLimit, so difficulty can rise freely but cannot
  fall below the powLimit floor — a regtest artifact, not a mainnet property; I raised difficulty ~30×
  above floor (to 7.17e-09) where needed to get clean downside numbers.

## Vectors attempted

### A7 — Difficulty-raising griefing (minimal-gap blocks)
Method: from ASERT anchor, mined 20 then 50/200 consecutive blocks at 1 s spacing (each block.time = prev+1),
recorded `bits`/`difficulty`.
RESULT: **CONFIRMED (works, but self-limiting)**.
- Per minimal-gap block: **difficulty ×1.0173 (+1.73%)**. Measured 19-block ratio 1.3850 vs predicted
  `2^(89·19/3600)=1.3848`. **~40 minimal-gap blocks to double difficulty** (`3600/89`).
- To spike difficulty for everyone, the attacker must MINE those blocks (own hashrate), and minimal-gap
  blocks are the *normal* fast-block case — there is no timestamp trick that raises difficulty without
  actually producing blocks fast. So A7 "raise difficulty to orphan competitors" reduces to "have more
  hashrate," which is just majority mining. The cap is **neutral** to A7 (cap limits FUTURE drift, not
  minimal gaps). PR#214: **neutral**.

### D3 — Selfish mining tuned to ASERT
Method: 2-node partition (honest vs evil, same image). Evil withheld a private chain while honest mined
public blocks; evil released a heavier chain on reconnect.
RESULT: **CONFIRMED orphaning**. Honest mined 2 public blocks (h124-125); evil withheld 4 (h124-127);
on release honest reorged and its 2 blocks became a `valid-fork` (orphaned, branchlen=2). Honest work
on those blocks earned **0** canonical reward; evil captured all 4 canonical blocks of the interval.
- Eyal-Sirer relative-revenue (computed): alpha=25% → 0.195 (loss at gamma=0; break-even at gamma=0.5);
  **alpha=33% → 0.327 (gamma=0) / 0.379 (gamma=0.5, x1.15 PROFIT)**; **alpha=45% → 0.652/0.681 (x1.45-1.51 PROFIT)**.
  Threshold ≈ 1/3 at gamma=0, falling toward 0 as the attacker's relay/eclipse advantage (gamma) grows.
- **Does ASERT's fast retarget make selfish mining MORE or LESS profitable?** Steady-state revenue ratio
  is the **same** Eyal-Sirer value (it is hashrate-share driven, not retarget-driven). The half-life=3600 s
  fast retarget only changes the *transient*: by orphaning honest blocks the attacker suppresses the
  effective block rate, ASERT eases difficulty quickly, so the attacker reaches the new (cheaper) equilibrium
  **sooner** than on Bitcoin's 2-week retarget. Net: **marginally MORE attractive, not less** — fast retarget
  shortens the loss-making warm-up, it does not raise the threshold. PR#214 cap: **neutral** (selfish mining
  doesn't depend on future-timestamp freedom).

### D4 — Time-bandit / deep-reorg + double-spend
Method 1 (double-spend, cap-OFF): built a 120-block mature chain; evil paid honest merchant 50 BTX,
confirmed it (honest sees conf=1); evil partitioned, `invalidateblock`+`abandontransaction` to drop the
payment, mined a heavier 3-block fork (to h123) excluding the payment, reconnected.
RESULT: **DOUBLE-SPEND CONFIRMED**. Honest reorged to the heavier chain; the payment tx is **not in the
active chain** (`getrawtransaction` shows no blockhash); merchant's spendable balance dropped to ~1 BTX
(change only), `listunspent` shows **no 50-BTX output**. Merchant lost 50 BTX.
- Wallet UX note (low/med): immediately after the reorg the honest wallet still displayed the orphaned
  payment as `confirmations=3` with empty `walletconflicts` (because evil simply *excluded* the tx rather
  than broadcasting a conflicting spend of the same input). A merchant reading naive `gettransaction`
  confirmations would be **misled into thinking the payment still has 3 confs**. The funds are gone from
  the UTXO set; only a spend attempt / `listunspent` reveals it. Mitigation: merchants must check UTXO
  presence, not just wallet confirmation count; consider improving conflict detection for "excluded"
  (non-conflicting-spend) reorgs.

Method 2 (is the reorg CHEAPER thanks to ASERT difficulty drops?): from a high-difficulty tip (7.17e-09),
mined 10 fork blocks two ways and compared chainwork accrual.
RESULT: **NO free discount — empirically a wash**.
- FAST timestamps (1 s gaps): end difficulty 8.51e-09, chainwork gain = **333** units.
- SLOW timestamps (3000 s gaps, lowers difficulty): end difficulty crashed to floor 2.33e-10,
  chainwork gain = **71** units (= **21.3%** of FAST).
- Why: claiming slow blocks to depress fork difficulty ALSO slashes the chainwork the fork accrues per
  block. Reorg requires out-accumulating honest chainwork; a low-difficulty fork accrues proportionally
  less work, so it does NOT overtake any cheaper. Additionally the **first replacement block's difficulty
  is fixed by the shared fork-point timestamp** (honest history) — the attacker cannot discount it at all.
  => Deep reorg remains a **chainwork race needing sustained >50% hashrate**; ASERT difficulty drops are
  **neutral** to reorg cost. (1-conf double-spend needs only a momentary work lead, achievable below 50%
  by luck/withholding, as demonstrated.)

Method 3 (does the cap change reorg economics?): raise-then-drop on cap-OFF vs cap-ON.
RESULT: **cap THROTTLES the difficulty-drop lever**.
- Cap-OFF: a single block claiming a 7200 s gap drops difficulty ×3.93 (`2^((7200−90)/3600)`); larger
  gaps drop it further (×15.7 at 14400 s) — unbounded by the MTP rule, limited only by
  MAX_FUTURE_BLOCK_TIME (real_clock+7200). Difficulty crashed to floor in **2 blocks**.
- Cap-ON: requested 7200 s gaps were **clamped to ≤ prev_MTP+3600** (observed block.time pinned, e.g.
  requested 1296710252 → accepted 1296699448). Max difficulty drop per cap-limited block ≈ **×1.97**
  (`2^((3600−90)/3600)`), and only as fast as MTP itself advances. The cap **prevents manufacturing an
  artificially low-difficulty fork** via slow-timestamp blocks.

## NEW / unmitigated findings
- **Reorg wallet-confirmation desync after "exclusion" reorg** | severity low-med | repro: pay merchant,
  confirm (conf≥1), attacker reorgs with a heavier chain that simply omits the tx (no conflicting spend
  broadcast); honest wallet keeps showing stale `confirmations` (saw conf=3) with empty `walletconflicts`
  until a spend is attempted. UTXO is actually gone. proposed mitigation: detect "tx no longer in active
  chain and not in mempool" and surface as conflicted/abandoned in `gettransaction`; document merchant
  guidance to verify via `gettxout`/`listunspent`, not confirmation count.
- (No new *consensus*-level break found in D3/D4/A7 beyond known hashrate-threshold attacks.)

## What PR #214's cap helps / hurts / neutral
- **A7 (difficulty raise):** NEUTRAL — cap limits forward drift, not minimal gaps.
- **D3 (selfish mining):** NEUTRAL — does not depend on future-timestamp freedom; fast ASERT retarget is
  the (minor) aggravator, and that is inherent to the half-life choice, not the cap.
- **D4 (time-bandit difficulty-drop):** **HELPS.** The cap bounds the single-block difficulty-drop lever
  from ×3.93+ (uncapped, 7200 s) / unbounded to ×1.97 (one half-life). It blocks the "manufacture a
  cheap low-difficulty window by claiming slow blocks" trick. It does NOT change the core reorg cost
  (chainwork race), because that was already ASERT-neutral (Method 2). And it does not harm liveness:
  honest late blocks up to the cap (tested 3000 s gap) are accepted; over-cap timestamps are clamped at
  mining time, not rejected as invalid (no stall).

## Thresholds (small-chain, low absolute hashrate)
- **MTP / timestamp-cap control:** mine **6 of the trailing 11 blocks** (a burst) to set the MTP and thus
  the cap ceiling — independent of long-run share. (median-of-11.)
- **Selfish-mining revenue:** alpha **> 1/3** (gamma=0); → 0 as relay/eclipse advantage gamma → 1.
- **1-conf double-spend:** momentary chainwork lead — achievable **below 50%** by luck/withholding (demonstrated).
- **N-conf deep reorg:** sustained **>50%** hashrate; ASERT-neutral.

## SOUND mitigations (and one anti-recommendation)
- **D4 difficulty-drop lever:** PR#214's future-MTP-drift cap is the right, sound mitigation and already
  bounds it to one half-life. A *cumulative windowed* future-drift cap (per LEARNINGS direction) would
  further reduce the steady-state ratchet without per-block over-tightening — SOUND.
- **Selfish mining (D3):** **DO NOT attempt a consensus-layer "fix."** It is fundamentally unsolvable at
  the consensus layer for alpha>1/3; proposed cures (e.g., random/uniform tie-breaking, published-timestamp
  penalties, "first-seen" enforcement) are unenforceable in a permissionless network and create new DoS /
  block-withholding-detection false-positive vectors — a mitigation **worse than the disease**. Correct
  responses are operational: maximize honest connectivity to drive gamma→0 (raising the threshold toward
  1/3), monitor orphan rate, and rely on confirmation depth for high-value settlement.
- **ASERT fast retarget aggravating the selfish transient:** the half-life (3600 s) is a deliberate
  responsiveness choice; lengthening it to blunt selfish mining would re-introduce the oscillation/lag
  problems ASERT was chosen to fix. Not worth it — NEUTRAL/leave as is.
- **Reorg double-spend generally:** ASERT-neutral, hashrate-bound; only real defense is confirmation depth
  + the wallet-desync UX fix above.

## Refuted / robust (so others don't repeat)
- "ASERT difficulty drop lets you deep-reorg cheaply" — **REFUTED.** Lowering fork difficulty via slow
  timestamps cuts accrued chainwork proportionally (measured 71 vs 333 over 10 blocks); the first reorg
  block's difficulty is fixed by the shared fork-point timestamp. Reorg stays a >50% chainwork race.
- "Path of intermediate timestamps lets a withholder pre-discount future difficulty" — **REFUTED.** ASERT
  is absolute-anchor / memoryless (`pow.cpp:1563-1564`); only the tip block's timestamp matters.
- "ASERT's fast retarget raises the selfish-mining threshold above Bitcoin's" — **REFUTED.** Same
  steady-state threshold; fast retarget marginally *helps* the attacker (shorter transient).
