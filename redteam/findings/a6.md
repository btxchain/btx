## 1. SIGN of ASERT response (unambiguous, from identical baseline)
- min-gap (block.time=prev_mtp+1) ⇒ next difficulty UP ×1.0087/block.
- future-cap (block.time=prev_mtp+3600) ⇒ next difficulty DOWN ×0.848/block.
⇒ small-gap/past ⇒ HARDER; large-gap/future ⇒ EASIER. Standard ASERT. **REFUTES external #2/#6 (they had the sign backwards).** Cap correctly clamps over-cap (gap+5000 → +3600).

## 3. Exploitable for profit? NO.
Future-cap lowers difficulty ONLY by advancing block.time into the future, hard-bounded by MAX_FUTURE_BLOCK_TIME=real_now+7200. With node clock pinned at real "now": first block catches MTP to the cap, every subsequent block reverts to min-gap and difficulty climbs straight back up (8.56e-10→9.35e-10). Low-difficulty and high-block-rate are MUTUALLY EXCLUSIVE: holding difficulty down needs +3600s MTP/block, but past real time the attacker advances MTP only as fast as real time (~1 blk/hr vs 40 honest). No net-downward bias, no profit. (Corroborates a1/a2/b7: bounded, unprofitable under realistic clock.)

## 4. BIP94 is load-bearing
BIP94 (time-timewarp-attack, MAX_TIMEWARP=600) enforces block.time ≥ prev.time−600 ⇒ neutralizes oscillation: after a +3600 block raises prev.time, the next past block can't be dated >600s earlier. ~half of alternating min-gap blocks were force-clamped up; a prev.time−5000 attempt clamped to max(prev_mtp+1, prev.time−600).

## DECISION — no past-drift bound (don't repeat / don't add)
Keep the fix FUTURE-ONLY. A symmetric/past-drift bound (block.time ≥ prev_mtp−X) adds NO security (past-dating already hurts attacker + BIP94 covers it) and carries LIVENESS RISK (rejects honest blocks legitimately a bit behind after a slow period). External #2/#6 REFUTED. Recommendation logged for the PR design.
