## STRUCTURAL MAP — the x1c pattern is PERVASIVE in VerifyCT (code analysis, file:line)

The whole live-m=1 CT relation is verified through a chain of **prover-supplied binding
digests** that are recomputed over values that are themselves **recovered from the prover's own
commitment**, never from the public statement:

- **`round1_aux_binding_digest`** (verify @3488): recomputed over `recovered_aux_tmsg` (= the
  prover's `aux_commitment.t_msg`, recovered at @1131 `RecoverFullAuxMessageCommitments` — for
  most slots literally copied from `proof.aux_commitment.t_msg`), compared to the prover's digest.
- **`pre_h2_binding_digest`** (@3541): over `recovered_aux_tmsg` + `recovered_w0_commitment_accs`
  (recovered from `proof.z` / `proof.w0_residue_accs`), vs prover's digest.
- **`post_h2_binding_digest`** (@3675): over `recovered_aux_tmsg[PsiSlot]` + the verifier-computed
  `lhs`, vs prover's digest. The prover (@2977) sets `framework_omega` AND picks the Psi aux
  message (@2969) so that the recovered `lhs` equals whatever it committed. ⇒ self-referential.
- **`coin_opening.binding_digest`** (@1944): over `recovered_binding_acc` (a reduction of the
  prover's `z`/`z_coin`/`z_amount`/residues), vs the prover's digest.
- **omega / framework_omega `!IsZero()` gates** (@3589 / @3685): omega is the weak-opening MSIS
  accumulator (@3078 prover) and framework_omega is the masked relation value — both
  prover-supplied, both compared only to prover-recovered accumulators, AND skipped entirely
  when the prover sets them to 0 (x1c: zeroing them still accepts the balance forge).

KEY OBSERVATION (membership): the compressed-public-X (`ComputeCtCompressedPublicX`, the
one-out-of-many combiner over the FULL public ring/anon_set/coin_rings/account_rings) is called
ONLY in the PROVER (ct_proof.cpp:2865). **The verifier NEVER recomputes it** — in the framework
relation `framework_sum += f[selector_slot]·f[x_slot]` (@3691) both `f[selector_slot]` and
`f[x_slot]` are recovered from the prover's aux commitment (@1131), not derived from the public
ring. So membership binding rests on the same self-referential digests, not an independent
recomputation. (This is the structural twin of x1c's "verifier never recomputes the carry".)
**HOWEVER — empirically this site is NOT forgeable (unlike the balance carry):** the D2/E1
forges below show a two-hot / non-binary selector is REJECTED at step 11. The reason the digest
DOES bind here but NOT for balance: `f[selector]`, `f[x]`, and the amount residues are
algebraically coupled to the masking responses `z` (pinned by the round1/pre-h2 digests +
weak-opening + norm bounds), so the prover cannot pre-commit a Psi/digest that keeps `lhs`
self-consistent under a tampered selector/coin. The balance carry is the lone term the prover
can move freely (it lives only in `proof.h2`, and the digest binds the verifier's `lhs` to that
same prover-chosen `h2`). So the structural pattern is pervasive but the EXPLOITABLE instance is
singular = x1c.

## SEVERITY / NEW HOLES

**NO NEW structural hole found beyond x1c.** Despite the verifier's pervasive "bind to a
prover-supplied digest" pattern, the selector-binarity, one-of-many membership, and per-coin
committed-amount invariants each have an enforcer that survives BOTH x1c's carry-zeroing AND the
maximal direct-h2-zeroing bypass:
- They are first caught at step 1 (h2 carry) because each perturbs `live_y_sum`.
- When step 1 is fully neutralized (force_h2_zero), they are caught at step 11 (post-h2 framework
  binding digest), which is INDEPENDENT of h2.

The post-h2 digest works for these (but NOT for balance) because the framework relation
`lhs = α(framework_sum − c·f[G] − c²·h2) + bin_check + f[Psi]` algebraically couples the recovered
selector residue `f[sel]` (binarity term `f[sel]²+c·f[sel]`), the recovered selected-account row
`f[x]` (membership term `f[sel]·f[x]`), and the recovered amount residues (coin-opening) to the
masking responses `z` — which are themselves pinned by the round1/pre-h2 digests, the
weak-opening accumulator, and the norm bounds. The prover cannot pre-commit a `Psi`/digest that
makes `lhs` self-consistent once it injects a non-binary selector, a two-hot selector, or a
coin/amount mismatch. The ONLY relation term the prover can freely zero without breaking the
post-h2 digest is the standalone value carry in `h2` itself (the `c²·h2` term cancels because the
prover commits h2 and the digest binds the verifier's `lhs` to the prover's own h2) — which is
EXACTLY x1c's balance hole and nothing more.

⇒ **x1c's CRITICAL is SHARP and SINGULAR: it is specifically the value-conservation (balance)
carry that VerifyCT trusts in `proof.h2` without independent recomputation. The neighbouring
soundness invariants (binarity, membership, coin-amount) are independently enforced and were NOT
