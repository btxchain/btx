## Refuted / robust (so others don't repeat)

- **ASERT rounding-bias / ratchet-down attack â€” does NOT work.** ASERT is stateless and
  path-independent; mean-preserving timestamp oscillation produced an exactly periodic, drift-free
  target (empirical: `2100fb9câ†”2100f757` for 20 blocks). No accumulation channel exists.
- **ASERT integer overflow/underflow â€” none found.** `__int128` + int64 saturation + 256-bit
  saturating mul/shift + powLimit/min-1 clamps make every extreme input (huge/negative/zero
  time_diff, huge/negative height_diff) safe; no assert reachable in `CalculateMatMulAsertTarget`.
- **Anchor cannot be biased without a deep reorg.** Anchor is `GetAncestor(nMatMulAsertHeight)` on the
  active chain; anchor.nBits/anchor.time are fixed once buried. On mainnet anchor=50000 (deeply
  buried) â€” biasing it requires reorging below height 50000, infeasible. (One-time anchor.time bias
  exists only at the activation block itself, before it is buried.)
- **Boundary heights (retune/retune2/half-life-upgrade) â€” no off-by-one, no reorg activation.** They
  are consensus constants (not chain-derived), guarded by `ValidateMatMulAsertParams` ordering/fail-
  closed checks; the upgrade-boundary re-anchor at h20 was exact in regtest.
