# f2 — FIELD / ALGEBRAIC / MATRIX-GENERATION structure: NTT, structured-matmul, Strassen, GPU/ASIC asymmetry

Scope (number-theory/algorithms angle, distinct from e1's amortization and b1's forgery):
can an attacker multiply the consensus matmul BELOW the assumed naive n³ cost by exploiting
the FIELD (p=2^31−1 Mersenne), the MATRIX-GENERATION structure (SharedFromSeed), or fast
matmul algorithms (Strassen/NTT) — for an asymmetric mining advantage? And does the design
admit a hardware speedup so large it breaks the egalitarian-mining thesis?

Consensus params (mainnet, src/kernel/chainparams.cpp:150, consensus/params.h):
**n=512, transcript block b=16, noise rank r=8, Freivalds k=2, p=2^31−1.**

## Bottom line
- **NTT is NOT applicable** — for two independent reasons (one number-theoretic, one
  structural). NO sub-cubic single-product win from the field. Q1 refuted.
- **Matrix-generation has NO exploitable structure.** A,B from `FromSeed` are per-entry
  SHA256 oracles ⇒ full rank, full Toeplitz-displacement rank, all rows distinct ⇒ no
  Toeplitz/circulant/low-rank/structured-matmul shortcut. Q2 refuted.
- **Strassen gives a real but MODEST constant edge (~1.3–1.6×) on the ONE-TIME A·B** — but
  e1 already amortizes that dense product to ~free, so Strassen adds ~nothing on top of e1.
  Marginal LOW finding only if a miner does NOT implement e1. Q3 = LOW.
- **★ GPU/ASIC asymmetry is the REAL field/structure break (Q4): n=512 dense INT32 GEMM over
  a Mersenne field is the single most hardware-optimized kernel in existence** (GPU tensor/
  integer cores, TPUs). The design SHIPS Metal+CUDA backends. This is a 10²–10³× commodity-GPU-
  vs-CPU asymmetry ⇒ the "egalitarian / ASIC-resistant matmul-PoW" thesis is FALSE by
  construction. **MED–HIGH** (it's a design property, not a bug; matches published prior art).

## Q1 — Field p = 2^31−1: NTT applicability (REFUTED, two independent reasons)

**Reason A (number-theoretic — Mersenne is NTT-hostile):** a radix-2 NTT of length L needs a
primitive L-th root of unity, i.e. L | (p−1). Here
```
p−1 = 2^31 − 2 = 2 · 3² · 7 · 11 · 31 · 151 · 331
```
The **2-adic valuation of p−1 is exactly 1** (`/tmp/rtF2/ntt_matmul.py`). ⇒ the ONLY power-of-2
transform length supported is **2**. There is NO root of unity of order 512 (512 ∤ p−1), no
order-1024, etc. A power-of-2 NTT at the consensus n=512 is **impossible** in F_p. (This is
precisely why post-quantum/FHE/STARK systems use NTT-FRIENDLY primes — Proth/Solinas like
Goldilocks 2^64−2^32+1 has 2-adicity 32 — and NOT Mersenne 2^31−1. The Mersenne-number
transform variants (NMNT) in the literature transform over a *different* ring and are still
convolution-only — see Reason B.)

**Reason B (structural — NTT accelerates CONVOLUTION, not GEMM):** even on a perfectly
NTT-friendly field, an NTT computes a length-L cyclic **convolution** in O(L log L). A matmul
A(n×n)·B(n×n) is n² independent dot-products — it is **not** a convolution and does not reduce
to one transform. Dense GEMM stays Θ(n³) (or Θ(n^2.807) Strassen) regardless of NTT-friendliness;
NTT buys nothing for GEMM. So Q1 fails *twice over* for p=2^31−1.

⇒ **No NTT-based asymmetric advantage. The choice of a Mersenne field neither enables nor is
needed for the (e1) amortization break; the field's fast Mersenne reduction (field.cpp
reduce64) speeds honest and attacker equally.** RESULT: REJECTED.

## Q2 — Matrix-generation structure (SharedFromSeed) (REFUTED)

`SharedFromSeed`/`FromSeed` (matrix.cpp:491) fills EVERY entry independently:
`A[r][c] = field::from_oracle(seed_a, r*n + c)` = (SHA256(seed‖index) & MODULUS), rejection-
sampled. seed_a/seed_b = `DeterministicMatMulSeed(prev,height,which)=SHA256(prev‖height‖which)`
(pow.cpp:47). So A,B are **per-entry independent pseudorandom field elements** — a dense random
matrix oracle, no algebraic relation between entries.

Empirical (`/tmp/rtF2/structure.py`, faithful from_oracle reimpl, 32×32 sample over F_p):
- **rank = 32 / 32** (full rank ⇒ no low-rank factorization shortcut)
- **Toeplitz displacement rank = 32** (A − Z·A·Zᵀ is full-rank ⇒ NOT Toeplitz/Hankel-structured;
  a structured matrix would have displacement rank ≤ ~2)
- **distinct rows = 32 / 32** (no repeated rows/columns to dedupe)

⇒ no Toeplitz/circulant/Vandermonde/low-displacement-rank structure, no repeated rows, no
algebraic relation ⇒ no structured-matrix (super-fast) multiply applies. (This corroborates
b1's "A,B full-rank from per-entry oracle" and e1's "A,B full-entropy".) RESULT: REJECTED.

## Q3 — Practical sub-cubic (Strassen/Winograd) at n=512 (LOW)

n=512 = 2^9 is ideally shaped for Strassen recursion. Op-count model (`/tmp/rtF2/ntt_matmul.py`,
field mul weighted 3× a field add/sub — mul = mul64 + Mersenne fold; add = add + cond-sub):

| Strassen leaf | field mults | field add/sub | weighted cost vs naive |
|---------------|------------:|--------------:|-----------------------:|
| 512 (=naive)  | 134,217,728 | 133,955,584   | 1.000 |
| 256           | 117,440,512 | 118,161,408   | 0.877 |
| 128           | 102,760,448 | 105,201,664   | 0.771 |
| 64            |  89,915,392 |  95,367,168   | 0.680 |
| 32            |  78,675,968 |  89,396,224   | 0.606 |

⇒ Strassen down to a ~32–64 leaf cuts the single dense product cost ~**1.3–1.6×** (mults drop
~1.7×; the extra O(n²) adds eat into the integer-field gain, so net ~1.5×; real wall-clock less
because of worse cache/SIMD behavior). The cost model and reference solver are NAIVE n³
(matrix.cpp `MultiplyBlocked` / `operator*`, accelerated_solver), so a Strassen miner DOES get
an edge over a naive honest miner — **but only on the dense A·B**, and **e1 already reduces the
per-nonce dense product to ZERO** (per-nonce work is O(n²r) low-rank corrections, not a GEMM).
So Strassen helps only the ONE-TIME A·B precompute (e1: <5% of a block's cost) ⇒ negligible on
top of e1, and a ~1.5× edge for a non-e1 miner. **Severity LOW** (subsumed by e1; the n³ cost
model is indeed naive-assuming, but the dominant break is e1's amortization, not Strassen).
RESULT: minor edge, REJECTED as a standalone significant vector.

## Q4 — ★ GPU/ASIC asymmetry breaks the egalitarian thesis (MED–HIGH, design-level)

The consensus work unit is **n=512 dense INT32 matrix multiply over a Mersenne field**. This is
*the* canonical kernel that two decades of GPU/TPU/tensor-core silicon were built to maximize:
- Integer/tensor GEMM throughput on a commodity GPU exceeds a CPU core by **~100–1000×**; the
  work is embarrassingly parallel (n² independent dots, perfectly tiled), fits in GPU SRAM at
  512², and the Mersenne reduction is a couple of cheap shifts/adds per MAC.
- The design itself **ships Metal + CUDA mining backends** (accelerated_solver.h: `requested_metal`,
  `requested_cuda`, `cuda_generated_inputs`, GPU input generation; backend_capabilities.h:
  METAL/CUDA) — i.e. GPU mining is a first-class, intended path, not a fringe optimization.
- **Prior art confirms this is inherent, not incidental:** matmul-PoW projects (e.g. "Pearl/PRL")
  explicitly *market* matmul because "a matmul ASIC is just a tensor accelerator" — i.e. they
  concede a matmul-PoW maps directly onto GPU/TPU/ASIC tensor hardware. The academic line
  ("Proofs of Useful Work from Arbitrary Matrix Multiplication", eprint 2025/685) frames matmul
  as *useful* work precisely BECAUSE specialized hardware does it best. A PoW whose work unit is
  the most-optimized commodity-accelerator kernel cannot be egalitarian: GPU/tensor-ASIC owners
  dominate CPU miners by orders of magnitude.

⇒ **The "egalitarian / ASIC-resistant" matmul-PoW thesis is false for BTX.** A CPU-only honest
miner is non-competitive vs a GPU miner (>100×), and a tensor-ASIC widens it further. This is a
DESIGN property (severity MED–HIGH as a thesis break) rather than an implementable "exploit
script" — but it is the genuine field/structure asymmetry the mission asked for, and it
**compounds e1**: e1's per-nonce O(n²r) low-rank corrections are EVEN MORE GPU/ASIC-friendly
(tiny rank-8 kernels) than dense GEMM ⇒ a GPU+e1 miner's edge over a naive CPU miner is the
*product* (≈12.8× × GPU factor), not the sum.

## Interaction with the e1 nonce-in-seed fix
- The proposed e1 fix (fold nonce into seed_a/seed_b ⇒ A·B changes every attempt, restoring
  Θ(n³)/attempt) **does NOT touch Q1/Q2/Q4**: the field stays Mersenne (still NTT-hostile, fine),
  A,B stay per-entry dense oracles (still unstructured, fine), and the per-attempt work becomes a
  genuine dense n³ GEMM — which is EXACTLY the GPU/tensor-core kernel. ⇒ **the e1 fix makes the
  GPU/ASIC asymmetry WORSE/more central, not better:** it removes the low-rank amortization but
  re-establishes a pure dense-GEMM-per-nonce PoW, the most GPU-favorable possible work unit.
- The e1 fix does neutralize the (already-minor) Strassen edge differently: Strassen would then
  apply to the per-nonce dense product, giving a non-e1-aware honest miner a ~1.5× disadvantage
  vs a Strassen miner *every nonce* — so **post-e1-fix, bump Strassen from LOW toward LOW-MED**
  (the cost model's naive-n³ assumption becomes per-nonce-relevant again). Recommend the cost/
  difficulty model explicitly assume Strassen-class n^2.807, or accept the ~1.5× as tolerated.

## NEW / unmitigated findings
- **MED–HIGH (design): matmul-PoW is NOT egalitarian — n=512 INT32 GEMM is the most GPU/TPU/
  tensor-ASIC-optimized kernel in existence; the node ships Metal+CUDA backends.** repro:
  conceptual + prior-art (Pearl/PRL, eprint 2025/685) + backend code (accelerated_solver.h,
  backend_capabilities.h METAL/CUDA). GPU vs CPU ≈10²–10³×; tensor-ASIC larger. proposed

  fairness); (b) if egalitarianism is a hard requirement, matmul-PoW is the wrong primitive
  (memory-hard PoW would be needed) — out of scope for a hardening PR, but a thesis-level caveat.
- **LOW (algorithmic, interacts with e1 fix): the n³ cost/PoW-fairness model is naive-assuming;
  Strassen/Winograd to a 32–64 leaf gives a ~1.3–1.6× field-work edge** at n=512 over a naive
  honest miner. Subsumed by e1 today (dense product is amortized away); becomes per-nonce-relevant
  AFTER the e1 nonce-in-seed fix. repro: `python3 /tmp/rtF2/ntt_matmul.py`. proposed mitigation:
  assume Strassen-class lower bound in any difficulty/fairness reasoning, or accept ~1.5× as a
  tolerated constant (it does not break consensus, only fairness by a small factor).

## Refuted / robust (so others don't repeat)
- **NTT-based sub-cubic matmul over F_p (p=2^31−1) — DOES NOT WORK.** (a) 2-adicity(p−1)=1 ⇒ no
  power-of-2 root of unity ≥2 ⇒ no length-512 radix-2 NTT exists in F_p; (b) NTT accelerates
  convolution, not dense GEMM, on ANY field. Mersenne field is NTT-hostile AND irrelevant to GEMM.
  Fast Mersenne reduction (field.cpp reduce64) speeds honest+attacker equally. (`/tmp/rtF2/ntt_matmul.py`)
- **Structured-matrix (Toeplitz/circulant/low-rank/repeated-row) shortcut on A,B — DOES NOT WORK.**
  `FromSeed` is a per-entry SHA256 oracle ⇒ A is full rank (32/32), full Toeplitz displacement rank
  (32/32), all rows distinct. No structure to exploit. (`/tmp/rtF2/structure.py`) Corroborates b1/e1.
- **Strassen is NOT a meaningful standalone break** — only ~1.5× constant, and subsumed by e1's
  amortization (which removes the dense product entirely). Listed as LOW for completeness.
